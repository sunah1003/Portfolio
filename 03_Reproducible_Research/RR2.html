Effects of Climate Events on Health and Economy in the United States over the year of 1950 and 2011

Sunah Park, 25. October 2018

1. SYNOPSIS

This report identifies the impact of severe weather events on both public health and economy for communities and municipalities in the United States.

For this purpose, historical data about severe weather events in the U.S. from 1950 to November 2011, which are available from the U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administrations (NOAA) strom database was used. This database include characteristics of major storms and weather events in the U.S. accompanied with the date and the place of occurrence as well as information about any health damages such as fatalities, injuries and economic damages of properties and crops.

Processing the data

For the analysis, the following seven variables from the original data base are considered: * 1. Type of event (Column name: EVTYPE) * 2. Number of Fatalities (Column name: FATALITIES) * 3. Number of Injuries (Column name: INJURIES) * 4. Property Damage (Column name: PROPDMG) * 5. Property Damage Multiplier (Column name: PROPDMGEXP) * 6. Crop Damage (Column name: CROPDMG) * 7. Crop Damage Multiplier (Column name: CROPDMGEXP).

        arrange(desc(prop))

Now we have two data frames of (1)df_human: for health damages (fatalities and injuries) and (2)df_econ: for economic damages (property and crop).

3. RESULTS

The results are shown in the graphics below.

# Visualization of the processed data
# Impact of the weather events on public health (fatalities and injuries): 
df_human$EVTYPE<-factor(df_human$EVTYPE, 
                levels=unique(as.character(df_human$EVTYPE)))
barchart(fat+inj~EVTYPE, data=df_human[1:7,],stack=TRUE,
         xlab="Event Type",
         ylab="Total number of Fatalities and Injuries",
         main="Impact of the weather events on public health",
         auto.key=list(space="top",text=c("Fatalities","Injuries")),
         scales=list(x=list(rot=90)))

# Impact of the weather events on economy (property and crop damages): 
df_econ$EVTYPE<-factor(df_econ$EVTYPE, 
                        levels=unique(as.character(df_econ$EVTYPE)))

barchart(prop+crop~EVTYPE, data=df_econ[1:7,],stack=TRUE,
         xlab="Event Type",
         ylab="Total damage in billion USD",
         main="Impact of the weather events on economy",
         auto.key=list(space="top",text=c("Property Damage","Crop Damage")),
         scales=list(x=list(rot=90)))

4. CONCLUSION

During the period of 1950 and 2011 in the U.S., Tornado caused the most impact on both in terms of Fatalities and Injuries. With respect to economic damage, Flood was the event that had the largest impact.
